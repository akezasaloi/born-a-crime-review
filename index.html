Born a Crime

An alien in my home

In this story, the main character "Trevor" is always judged by his neighbours and colleagues for being colored and not accepted
as a black due to apartheid laws. This makes it hard for him to belong where he lives hence the title ‘An alien in my home’

“Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women…
He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.”

Abel just like today's society expects women to be independent at a certain level and at the same time,
become submissive to their partners. A good example is in nowadays relationships where men
don't want to be asked to give the bare minimum even in courtship like pay bills on dates and still
expect women to respond to their every whim in an instant. They want women to work
and pay 50% of the family's expenses while taking care of the children with all household duties on a daily basis.

In the story Abel wants Patricia to act and live the way he wants because he believes that's the way it should be
in a marriage, relating it to the way his father treated his mother and his grandfather before him. He desires to
manipulate Patricia so that he can take away her audacity and turn her into a plain traditional wife
like the ones in his home place. Today's society also wants women to always give and follow the rules set by men
without question.

By Akeza Saloi
