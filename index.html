Born a crime review

An alien in my home

In this story, the main character "Trevor" is always judged by his neighbours and colleagues for being colored and not accepted as a black due to apartheid laws. This makes it hard for him to belong where he lives hence the title ‘An alien in my home’

Meet the author

Trevor Noah is a South African Comedian, author of Born a Crime, a memoir for his mother and bibliography of his life. He is known for hosting an American stand up talk show called “The Daily Show since 2015.

Review

“Abel wanted a traditional marriage with a traditional wife… but he never fell in love with subservient women… He only wants a woman who is free because his dream is to put her in a cage.”

Abel just like today's society expects women to be independent at a certain level and at the same time, become submissive to their partners. A good example is in nowadays relationships where men don't want to be asked to give the bare minimum even in courtship like pay bills on dates and still expect women to respond to their every whim in an instant. They want women to work and pay 50% of the family's expenses while taking care of the children with all household duties on a daily basis.

In the story Abel wants Patricia to act and live the way he wants because he believes that's the way it should be in a marriage, relating it to the way his father treated his mother and his grandfather before him. He desires to manipulate Patricia so that he can take away her audacity and turn her into a plain traditional wife like the ones in his home place.

Today's society also wants women to always give and follow the rules set by men without question. Some men want to know where their female partners went and they put restrictions on who they talk to and who they meet.In some countries especially in Africa and Asia they don't even want them to learn because they know if women learn they will outsmart them and fight for their independence.

By Akeza Saloi
